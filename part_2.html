Latin Language

Latin is an ancient Indo-European language that originated in the Italian Peninsula and was spoken by the Romans. It served as the foundation for the Romance languages, including Italian, French, Spanish, Portuguese, and Romanian. Latin was the language of literature, law, science, and the Catholic Church for centuries, influencing many modern languages and cultures. Though it is no longer spoken as a native language, it is still studied for its historical significance and its role in classical education.
